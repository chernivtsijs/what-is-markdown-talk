# Markdown

<!-- Slide 1 -->


---


<!-- Slide 2 -->

**Markdown** is a markup language with
_plain text formatting syntax_.

<!-- Slide 2 -->


---


<!-- Slide 3 -->

It can be converted to **HTML**
and many other formats.

<!-- Slide 3 -->


---


<!-- Slide 4 -->

Markdown is often used to:

* format readme files
* write messages
* create rich text using a text editor.

<!-- Slide 4 -->


---


<!-- Slide 5 -->

Used by:

* **GitHub**
* **StackOverflow**
* **Gitter**
* **Slack**
* **Gitlab**
* **Bitbucket**
* **README** in any project.

<!-- Slide 5 -->


---


<!-- Slide 6 -->

## History

<!-- Slide 6 -->


---


<!-- Slide 7 -->

**Year:** 2004
**Designer:** _John Gruber_
**Developer:** _Aaron Swartz_

<!-- Slide 7 -->


---


<!-- Slide 8 -->

The main goal:

> “to write using an _easy-to-read_,
> _easy-to-write_ plain text format.”

<!-- Slide 8 -->


---


<!-- Slide 8 -->

The key design goal is _readability_.
Markdown is _readable as-is_.

<!-- Slide 8 -->


---


<!-- Slide 9 -->

Its main inspiration a conventions
for marking up plain text in email.

<!-- Slide 9 -->


---


<!-- Slide 10 -->

_Gruber_ wrote a Perl script:
`Markdown.pl`.

<!-- Slide 10 -->


---


<!-- Slide 11 -->

## Standardization

<!-- Slide 11 -->


---


<!-- Slide 12 -->

There is no clearly defined
Markdown standard.

<!-- Slide 12 -->


---


<!-- Slide 13 -->

This has led to fragmentation.

<!-- Slide 13 -->


---


<!-- Slide 14 -->

Gruber said that complete
standardisation is a mistake:

> “Anyone has different needs.
> No one syntax would make all happy.”
> — _John Gruber_

<!-- Slide 14 -->


---


<!-- Slide 15 -->

## CommonMark

<!-- Slide 15 -->


---


<!-- Slide 16 -->

From 2012, a standardization process
has started.

<!-- Slide 16 -->


---


<!-- Slide 17 -->

In September 2014, _Gruber_ objected to
the usage of **Markdown** name.

<!-- Slide 17 -->


---


<!-- Slide 18 -->

It was rebranded as a new dialect
named **CommonMark**.

<!-- Slide 18 -->


---


<!-- Slide 19 -->

Announce a finalized 1.0 spec _in 2017_.

<!-- Slide 19 -->


---


<!-- Slide 20 -->

In 2017 **GitHub** released a
[formal specification](github.github.com/gfm)
of **Github Flavored Markdown**.

<!-- Slide 20 -->


---


<!-- Slide 21 -->

It follows the **CommonMark** spec.
